Our Mission

The Omaha Housing Initiative represents hope for hundreds of families facing housing instability in our community. While our city invests in long-term solutions, we're here to provide immediate relief and innovative pathways to homeownership.

Every dollar you contribute directly impacts real families, providing not just shelter, but stability, dignity, and the foundation for building better futures right here in Omaha.

Why Omaha Needs This Now

Housing costs in Omaha have surged while wages haven't kept pace. Working families - teachers, healthcare workers, service industry employees - are being priced out of stable housing. This isn't about homelessness; it's about the invisible struggle of families choosing between rent and groceries, between staying in Omaha or leaving the community they call home.

While Mayor Ewing's administration has committed significant resources to housing development along the streetcar corridor, there's an immediate gap that community action can fill. That's where we come in - bridging the gap between crisis and long-term solutions.

A Personal Story

Housing instability doesn't discriminate. It affects working families, single parents, veterans, and young adults just starting their careers. Behind every statistic is a human story of resilience, hope, and the fundamental need for a place to call home.

When you're worried about where you'll sleep next month, everything else becomes impossible. Your job performance suffers. Your kids' education suffers. Your health suffers. This initiative isn't just about housing - it's about restoring hope and giving families the foundation they need to thrive in Omaha.

This campaign was born from witnessing firsthand how housing challenges create ripple effects that touch every aspect of life. But it was also born from seeing the incredible strength of our Omaha community when we come together to solve problems. Your support today becomes someone else's new beginning tomorrow.

Every family deserves stability. Every child deserves a safe place to do homework. Every working adult deserves housing they can afford without sacrificing other necessities. Together, we can make that a reality for more families in our city.
